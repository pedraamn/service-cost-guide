Poison Ivy Removal Cost

Average pricing, method comparisons, and safety-first guidance for homeowners dealing with poison ivy.

Quick answer

Poison ivy removal typically costs $150 to $600, depending on the size of the infestation, removal method, and safety requirements. Small patches may cost less, while large or recurring infestations often require professional treatment and follow-up applications.

Introduction

Poison ivy is more than a landscaping nuisance—it’s a health hazard. The plant’s oil (urushiol) can trigger severe allergic reactions, and it can spread through tools, gloves, and clothing. Because of that risk, pricing varies based on infestation size, location, and whether professional safety measures are required.

Direct answer: How much does poison ivy removal cost?

Most homeowners can expect to pay $150 to $600 for professional poison ivy removal:

  • Small infestations: $150–$300
  • Medium infestations: $300–$500
  • Large or recurring infestations: $500–$1,000+

Many contractors charge a minimum service fee due to protective equipment, labor risk, and proper disposal requirements.

Poison ivy removal cost by yard size or infestation

  • Small patches (under ~100 sq ft): $150–$250
  • Moderate growth (~100–500 sq ft): $250–$500
  • Extensive growth (500+ sq ft): $500–$1,000+

Heavily rooted areas or vines climbing fences/trees may require multiple treatments, increasing the total cost.

Poison ivy removal cost by method

Removal method Typical cost range Notes
Manual removal $200–$500 Labor-intensive and higher exposure risk; may be used for vines/roots in tight areas.
Chemical treatment $150–$400 Often more affordable upfront; may require follow-up visits to prevent regrowth.
Combined methods $300–$800 Common for larger infestations where both visible growth and root systems need control.

What affects poison ivy removal pricing?

  • Severity of infestation: Dense, established growth costs more to remove.
  • Accessibility: Slopes, wooded areas, fences, and tight spaces increase labor.
  • Safety requirements: Protective gear and careful handling/disposal add cost.
  • Repeat treatments: Poison ivy often regrows if roots aren’t fully destroyed.
  • Local labor rates: Pricing varies by region and season.

Related questions

Is it cheaper to remove poison ivy yourself?

DIY removal can cost under $50 for supplies, but it carries real health risks. Improper removal often leads to regrowth—or exposure that can create medical costs and more work later.

How much does a poison ivy exterminator cost?

Specialized removal providers typically charge $300 to $800, depending on treatment type, site conditions, and whether follow-up visits are included.

How long does poison ivy take to come back after removal?

If the roots aren’t fully destroyed, poison ivy can regrow within weeks. Follow-up treatment is often the difference between “gone” and “back again.”

Expert insight from a lawn and safety perspective

Professional poison ivy removal is often worth the cost because it reduces exposure and improves long-term control. A common DIY failure is underestimating how easily urushiol spreads—especially through contaminated tools, gloves, and laundry.

Key takeaways

  • Poison ivy removal typically costs $150–$600.
  • Larger infestations and repeat growth increase pricing.
  • Chemical treatments are cheaper upfront but may require follow-up.
  • Professional removal can significantly reduce health risks.
